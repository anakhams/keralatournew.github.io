Welcome To The City Of Fests-THRISSUR !


Ever wanted to visit the marvellous Athirapally Waterfalls? Or seek blessings of Shree Krishna at the famous Guruvayur Temple? Or simply be hypnotised by the scintillating sight of sunset at the Chavakkad Beach? To make all these experiences come true, you need to plan a trip to Thrissur! God’s Own Country, Kerala, welcomes you to visit the quaint Thrissur district which is famous for some of the finest tourist attractions. Right from the divine church of Basilica of our Lady of Dolours to the Chettuva backwaters that connects to the Arabian Sea, there are many astounding visuals and tourist sights that make Thrissur a must visit.

    1.Vadakkunnathan Temple

    Vadakkunnathan Temple is one of the oldest and famous shrine of Lord Shiva that can be found in the Thrissur district of Kerala. The alluring temple decked in gold is easily one of the best places to visit in Thrissur and should be part of your list! Not just the fancy architecture but the intriguing murals that tell a tale of the deity bring out the cultural finesse of the state. You would have a good time exploring the temple.


    2.Shakthan Thampuran Palace

    The list of places to visit in Thrissur would be incomplete without the addition of Shakthan Thampuran Palace. The palace was constructed by Raja Ram Varma IC in the year 1791. The white-hued palace with its marvellous Dutch-style architecture would leave you dumbstruck. Further, there are relics and antiquities that would further pique your interest.


    3.Basilica of Our Lady of Dolours

    Adding to the lush green beauty of Thrissur is this towering church of Basilica of Our Lady of Dolours. Adored as the white gemstone of the city, it counts as one of the places to visit in Thrissur. It is a haven for peace and tranquillity. For those seeking peace, you would find it right here in the church with its alluring architecture and quaint vibe.


    4.Athirapally Waterfalls

    About 60 km from the main city of Thrissur is the famous Athirapally waterfall. The falls are often compared with Niagara Falls of the US and Canada. Well, one glance and you would be convinced with the comparison as it surely is India's Niagara Falls! What makes the falls scintillating are the greenscape surrounding it as the water flows down the Anamudi Mountains. It is easily one of the top places to visit near Thrissur.


    5.Guruvayur Sree Krishna Temple

    Guruvayur Sree Krishna Temple is not just one of the top places to visit in Thrissur but is also known to be famous worldwide. With a rich history and architectural beauty of the temple that is dedicated to Lord Guruvayurappan, the vicinity is always flooded with devotees and tourists. Due to its popularity, you might have to wait a little too much in the line but the whole experience is worth it.
